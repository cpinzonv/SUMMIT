Summit Brand Kit

The single source of truth for how Summit looks, sounds, and shows up — in the product, on social, in decks, and in every Claude Code prompt. All values extracted from the live app at learnsummit.app.

01 · Logo

The ridge mark

A mountain ridge drawn as one smooth, continuous gradient stroke — coral to sunset to teal — with softly rounded corners at every peak and valley. It is a single line, not a filled shape. Never rebuild it; use summit-logo.svg.

RuleValue
Gradient stroke versionDefault. Use on cream, white, and dark backgrounds.
White stroke versionOnly on gradient or photo backgrounds where the gradient stroke would lose contrast.
Drop shadow (product UI)drop-shadow(0 8px 18px rgba(255,120,80,.35))
Clear spaceKeep a margin of at least ½ the mark's height on all sides.
Minimum size24 px height on screen — below that the rounded corners blur together.
Wordmark lockupMark + "Summit" in Space Grotesk 700, letter-spacing −0.025em, filled with the hero gradient (see Typography).

Do

Scale it proportionally — stroke weight is part of the identity.

Place on cream, white, dark ink, or the hero gradient.

Use the standalone mark as avatar / favicon / app icon.

Don't

Fill the shape, outline it in a flat color, or change the gradient stops.

Rotate, stretch, add extra peaks, or redraw the path.

Put the gradient stroke on a busy photo — switch to the white version.

The wordmark

"Summit" set in Space Grotesk 700 with tight tracking, filled with the hero gradient. Sentence case always — never SUMMIT, never summit. The gradient runs 135° so the S starts warm and the final letters cool to sky.

Ambient glow background

The signature page atmosphere: cream base with four soft radial glows in the corners. This exact recipe is the app's background — reuse it for decks, social frames, and the site.

background-color:#f1e9e1;
background-image:
  radial-gradient(46rem 46rem at 4% -8%,  rgba(255,150,70,.22), transparent 70%),
  radial-gradient(48rem 48rem at 96% 4%,  rgba(74,150,214,.20), transparent 70%),
  radial-gradient(52rem 52rem at 78% 92%, rgba(255,110,110,.18), transparent 70%),
  radial-gradient(46rem 46rem at 10% 96%, rgba(58,190,184,.18), transparent 70%);

Higher-intensity variant (auth screens): same stops at alpha .5 / .42 / .40 / .38 plus a center glow radial-gradient(40rem 40rem at 48% 46%, rgba(255,200,160,.3), transparent).

05 · Glass components

The glass card

Summit's surfaces are frosted glass over the ambient glow: a white gradient at partial opacity, an 18px blur, a 1px near-white border, generous radius, and a soft warm shadow. This very page is built from the recipe.

background: linear-gradient(140deg, rgba(255,255,255,.95), rgba(255,255,255,.55));
border: 1px solid rgba(255,255,255,.9);
border-radius: 24px;
backdrop-filter: blur(18px);
box-shadow: 0 18px 40px -24px rgba(31,41,51,.25);
ElementSpec
Corner radius24px cards · 16px tiles · 12px inputs & code blocks
BordersAlways 1px. White-tinted on glass, rgba(31,41,51,.08) on white.
Hero CTAHero-gradient fill, white 600-weight label, pill or 12px radius, subtle warm shadow.
Destructive actionsHidden behind 3-dot menus — never a red button in the open.
MotionSmooth and brief (≈200ms ease). Auto-save quietly; no celebratory popups.
EmojisNever in product UI. Allowed sparingly in social captions and WhatsApp.

⚠ Email is the exception to everything above: no glassmorphism, no backdrop-filter — table layout and inline styles only (see templates.js).

06 · Voice & messaging

How Summit sounds

AssetLocked copy
TaglineYour whole semester, handled.

Fairness lineSemester pricing is one payment. It ends when the semester ends. Nothing to cancel.
PositioningOpinionated, zero-setup — Summit decides so students don't have to. The anti-Notion.

Voice is

Calm, concrete, benefit-first.

Confident without hype — show the feature, skip the adjectives.

Peer-to-peer: a student talking to students.

Fair and transparent — pricing plainly stated, "billed once" always visible.

Voice is never

Exclamation points in product or paywall copy.

Guilt-trip dismissals, fake urgency, dark patterns.

Corporate buzzwords ("revolutionize", "supercharge", "unleash").

Promises about unbuilt features (nothing ships in copy before it ships in code).
